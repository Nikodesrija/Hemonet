What is Blood ?

Blood is a vital bodily fluid that circulates through the arteries and veins, carrying essential substances such as oxygen, nutrients, hormones, and waste products to and from cells. It consists of several components:
Red blood cells (erythrocytes): These cells carry oxygen from the lungs to the rest of the body and carbon dioxide back to the lungs for exhalation.
White blood cells (leukocytes): These cells are part of the body's immune system, defending against infections and foreign invaders.
Platelets (thrombocytes): Fragments of cells that help with blood clotting, preventing excessive bleeding when there is an injury. Plasma: The liquid portion of blood, composed mostly of water, but also containing proteins, electrolytes, hormones, and waste products.

Blood plays a crucial role in maintaining homeostasis, regulating body temperature, pH balance, and transporting nutrients and oxygen to cells while removing waste products for elimination.

Tuberculosis

Tuberculosis (TB) is a bacterial infection primarily affecting the lungs but can also affect other parts of the body. TB can lead to complications such as internal bleeding, particularly in cases of disseminated TB where the infection spreads beyond the lungs to other organs. Severe bleeding may necessitate blood transfusions to replace lost blood and stabilize the patient.In very rare cases, severe TB illness may lead to multi-organ failure where blood transfusions are required.
